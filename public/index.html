The shift

Brand guidelines can’t talk
to AI agents.

AI agents are becoming the primary interface between consumers and brands. A 200-page PDF doesn’t negotiate with a shopping assistant. A Frontify library doesn’t adapt its tone for a 22-year-old in Lausanne versus a 55-year-old in Bern. The tools we have manage brand assets. What we need is something that embodies brand intelligence.

Today: static guidelines

PDFs, digital asset platforms, compliance checklists. One-directional. The brand transmits, the consumer receives. AI agents scrape and approximate. No understanding, no adaptation, no conversation.

.brand: living identity agent

An MCP server that any AI system can connect to. It doesn't describe a brand — it is one. It speaks, validates, generates, refuses, adapts, and learns. Not just colors and fonts — textures, sounds, feelings, stories, culture.

Theoretical foundation

The seventh typology
of dynamic identity.

Building on Irene van Nes' foundational work in Dynamic Identities, six typologies describe brand systems that change. None of them understand why they change. None can explain their own rules. None talk back.

01
Container
Fixed shape, variable content. The logo as frame. MTV, AOL.
02
Transformative
The logo itself mutates. Google Doodles.
03
Generative
Algorithmically created from parameters. Nordkyn, Casa da Música.
04
Reactive
Responds to real-time stimuli. MIT Media Lab.
05
Participative
Co-created with users. John Maeda at RISD.
06
Evolutive
Changes over time, lives and ages. Seed Media, EDP.

Conscious Identity

Not just dynamic — autonomous. Not just reactive — intelligent. A brand that actively perceives its audience, understands context, makes decisions about how to present itself, and learns from the outcome. The .brand agent acts as a persistent influencer, maintaining coherence across all receivers without requiring a human brand manager in the loop.

Strategic architecture — Nicolas Paupe

Four layers of
a living brand.

An operating system for brands that think. Not rigidity — a spectrum of creative freedom with hard edges.

1

Hybrid Brand Architecture

Clear parameters for artistic direction. AI operates autonomously within a defined segment of the brand universe — generating variations that are flexible without ever breaking recognition. Human creative direction sets the boundaries. AI explores within them.

2

Emotional & Authentic Targeting

The brand reads its audience. Consumer typologies, personal memories, cultural narratives shape how the brand presents itself. Positioning is triple: anthropological, sensory, and neuro. It doesn't manipulate — it resonates.

3

Live Monitoring & Optimization

Every interaction generates signal. A/B testing runs continuously. Content performance tracked in real time. The feedback loop is autonomous: signal in, optimization out, learning cycle back.

4

Incremental & Replicable Deployment

Deploy across every channel — digital, physical, spatial — with total coherence. Adapts to each client's nature, replicates for similar profiles. The last mile is automated but never generic.

Roadmap

What comes next.

From proof of concept to ecosystem.

Phase 1 — Now

Brand knowledge API. Structured data, semantic search, natural language queries. SBB proof of concept live on Cloudflare edge.

Phase 2

MCP brand agent. The API becomes a proper .brand file any AI system can connect to. Serves both AI tools and consumers.

Phase 3

Optimization loop. Every interaction generates signal. A/B testing, performance monitoring. The brand gets smarter.

Phase 4

Multi-channel deployment. Digital, physical, spatial. Total coherence. Automated but never generic.

Phase 5

The .brand ecosystem. File format spec, marketplace, inter-brand communication. Brand consciousness networks.
